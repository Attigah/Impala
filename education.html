Our Programme

Education Is the Cornerstone of Lasting Change

At the Impala Foundation, we believe that access to quality learning transforms not just individuals, but entire communities across generations. Our Educational Development Programme is designed to remove the barriers that prevent young people from accessing and completing their education.

We work directly with schools, learners, and families to provide scholarships, learning materials, tutoring support, and school infrastructure improvements. Our mentorship component connects students with professionals and role models who guide them toward higher education and meaningful career pathways.

We also support early childhood education initiatives, recognising that strong foundations in the early years are critical for lifelong success.

What We Do

Our Key Activities

01

Scholarships & Bursaries

We sponsor school and university fees for students whose families cannot afford them, ensuring no dream is cut short by financial hardship.

02

Mentorship & Career Guidance

Students are connected with professionals and role models who guide them toward higher education and meaningful career pathways.

03

Learning Materials

We provide textbooks, stationery, and digital tools so students can participate fully in their education without resource constraints.

04

Literacy & After-School Clubs

Our literacy clubs offer additional support outside the classroom, helping struggling students build confidence and academic skills.

05

School Infrastructure

From building desks and classrooms to improving sanitation facilities, we ensure schools are safe, dignified learning environments.

06

Skills & Vocational Training

At partner institutes like Dabokpa Technical, we support practical vocational skills that lead directly to employment and entrepreneurship.

Get Support

How to Apply for Educational Support

We welcome applications from eligible students throughout the year. Follow the steps below to apply.

Step 1 — Check Eligibility

You must be a student at an accredited Ghanaian university or technical institute from an underserved background with demonstrated financial need.

Step 2 — Complete the Form

Fill in the application form below with your personal details, academic information, and a brief personal statement explaining your circumstances.

Step 3 — Submit Supporting Documents

Attach your admission letter or student ID, academic transcript, and a proof of financial need (e.g., parent/guardian income statement).

Step 4 — Review & Interview

Our team reviews all applications and shortlisted candidates may be invited for a brief phone or in-person interview.

Step 5 — Notification

Successful applicants are notified within 4–6 weeks of submission and support begins at the start of the next academic term.
