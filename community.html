Community Development

Strong Communities Are Built on Shared Resources

The Impala Foundation's Community Development Programme focuses on strengthening the social, economic, and physical infrastructure of the communities we serve.

Through vocational skills training, we equip adults with practical, market-relevant skills — from fashion, welding and tailoring to digital literacy and entrepreneurship — enabling them to earn a sustainable income and contribute to local economies.

Our livelihood support initiatives help small traders and community cooperatives access micro-financing guidance and business development support. We also undertake community infrastructure projects, including clean water access and sanitation improvements.

Central to our approach is community ownership: we work alongside residents rather than simply for them.

Healthcare

Healthcare Is a Human Right

For many in underserved communities, essential healthcare remains out of reach. We bridge that gap.

The Impala Foundation's Healthcare Programme bridges critical gaps by bringing essential medical services, health education, and wellness support directly to communities that need them most.

We organise regular health outreach camps where community members can access free medical consultations, screenings, and referrals. Our health awareness campaigns tackle issues including maternal and child health, nutrition, malaria prevention, HIV/AIDS awareness, and mental health.

🏕️
Free Health Outreach Camps

Regular camps providing free consultations, screenings and referrals in underserved areas.

👶
Maternal & Child Health

Supporting mothers and children with nutrition guidance, ante-natal care access, and immunisation awareness.

📢
Health Awareness Campaigns

Malaria prevention, HIV/AIDS awareness, mental health campaigns and community health worker training.

Who It Serves:

Families, women, children, the elderly, and marginalised individuals in underserved communities across Ghana.

How the Nomination Works

Describe the Community

Tell us the name, location, and the main challenges the community faces — water, education, health, livelihoods, or all of the above.

Tell Us Who to Contact

Provide a community leader, elder, or contact person we can reach to learn more and verify the need.

Our Team Reviews

We assess nominations based on need, feasibility, and our current programme capacity. We may contact you or the community leader for more information.

Community Engagement

If selected, our team visits the community to conduct a needs assessment and begin co-designing the right intervention with residents.
